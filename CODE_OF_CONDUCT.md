# Code of Conduct

Baixada is a place to make and study card games together. Project spaces should
remain calm, specific, and welcoming to people with different backgrounds,
levels of experience, and relationships to the games.

## Expected conduct

- Discuss ideas and work, not a person's worth or motives.
- Give feedback that is direct, specific, and proportionate.
- Make room for questions, corrections, and unfamiliarity.
- Credit other people's work and respect licenses and attribution.
- Protect private information and stop an interaction when asked.
- Accept a maintainer's decision about scope without turning disagreement into
  hostility.

## Unacceptable conduct

Harassment, threats, stalking, discrimination, sexualized attention, deliberate
misgendering, sustained disruption, impersonation, doxxing, and publishing
private correspondence without consent are not accepted. Neither are bad-faith
reports intended to intimidate or silence another participant.

This policy applies in repositories, reviews, discussions, project-run social
spaces, and private exchanges that directly concern participation in Baixada.

## Reporting and response

Do not publish private or identifying evidence in an issue. For content or
accounts that violate GitHub's policies, use GitHub's private **Report
content** flow. For other project-space concerns, open a minimal issue in the
organization's `.github` repository requesting maintainer follow-up and omit
sensitive details.

Maintainers will review reports in confidence to the extent the available
GitHub tools allow. They may remove content, close or lock a discussion,
request a change in behavior, limit participation, or ban an account. Responses
will consider severity, context, prior conduct, and the safety of affected
people. Retaliation against a reporter or participant in a review is itself a
violation.

Maintainers who are personally involved in a report should recuse themselves
when another suitable maintainer is available.

This is an original project policy. It may be updated as the community and its
needs become clearer.
