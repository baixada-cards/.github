# Governance

Baixada uses a steward-led model suited to a small, volunteer project. Public
discussion informs decisions; responsibility for maintaining each repository
remains explicit.

## Roles

**Contributors** report problems, propose changes, review work, and submit
patches. No formal membership is required.

**Repository maintainers** review and merge changes, manage releases, respond
to security reports, and keep a repository's scope coherent. Maintainers may
delegate areas of ownership and may step down at any time.

**Lead steward** Elvis Sikora holds final responsibility for organization-wide
scope, repository creation or archival, maintainer appointments, brand rules,
licensing changes, and unresolved cross-repository decisions.

## Decisions

Routine choices are made in the issue or pull request where the work happens.
Substantial changes should record the problem, alternatives, tradeoffs, and
decision in a durable repository document or issue.

Maintainers seek informed agreement, but consensus is not a requirement.
Correctness, project scope, maintenance cost, and the integrity of the games
and research contracts take priority over vote counts. The responsible
maintainer decides repository-local matters; the lead steward resolves
cross-repository or disputed decisions.

## Maintainer access

Maintainer access is granted by the lead steward after sustained, constructive
work and evidence of sound judgment in the relevant area. Access follows least
privilege and may be narrowed or removed for inactivity, security concerns,
conduct violations, or a change in project needs.

No contributor is expected to remain available indefinitely. Ownership should
be documented well enough for another maintainer to continue the work.

## Transparency and private matters

Technical and product decisions are public by default. Vulnerability reports,
embargoed fixes, personal information, credentials, and conduct reports remain
private until disclosure is safe and appropriate.

Changes to this governance document are proposed by pull request and accepted
by the lead steward.
